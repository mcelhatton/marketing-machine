# Competitor Information

Use this information to understand MDD's competitive positioning. **NEVER mention competitors by name in customer-facing content.**

## Competitors

### TrueSpot
**Type:** Location/tracking competitor
**Competes With:** MDD Locate
**Notes:** Closest direct competitor on the location/tracking side.

### LoJack
**Type:** GPS-based vehicle tracking
**Competes With:** MDD Locate and VehicleVault

**Weaknesses:**
- GPS doesn't work well indoors where dealership cars often are
- MDD's Bluetooth approach is superior for indoor/lot environments

**Positioning:** "GPS-based solutions don't work well indoors where your cars actually are."

### Rapid Recon
**Type:** Reconditioning workflow software
**Competes With:** Recon Workflow

**Weaknesses:**
- Relies on manual status updates
- Someone has to remember to update the system

**Positioning:** "Other workflow tools rely on someone remembering to update the system. MDD's doesn't."

### Key Lock Box Systems Example KeyPer Systems or KeyTrak
**Type:** Legacy key management approach
**Competes With:** MDD Locate (Key Tracking)

**Weaknesses:**
- Only creates accountability, not location
- "Only as good as the last person that took the key"
- Keys can still end up on desks, in pockets, across the lot

**Positioning:** "Key boxes only track accountability — not where the key actually is right now."

## Competitive Positioning Rules

### NEVER Do This:
- Name competitors directly in customer-facing content
- Disparage competitor products by name
- Make direct comparison claims that could be challenged

### ALWAYS Do This:
- Use category references instead of names:
  - "legacy lot management systems"
  - "GPS-based solutions"
  - "manual workflow tools"

## MDD's Unfair Advantages

### 1. Bluetooth > GPS for Indoor/Lot Environments
GPS doesn't work well indoors. Dealership cars are often inside (service bays, detail shops, covered lots). Bluetooth provides accurate tracking where GPS fails.

### 2. LocateIQ Auto-Advances Workflows
No manual updates required. Because MDD knows where the car physically is, workflows update automatically. Competitors require staff to remember to log status changes.

### 3. Single Platform for Locate + Workflow + F&I

### 4. 10+ Years of Dealer Feedback

## Objection Handling

### "We already have key boxes"
Key boxes only create accountability — they tell you who took the key last, but not where the key is right now. Keys end up on desks, in pockets, across the lot. MDD shows real-time location.

### "We already have workflow software"
Most workflow software relies on manual updates. Someone has to remember to log that a car left the bay — and they often don't. MDD's LocateIQ updates automatically based on physical vehicle location.

### "GPS tracking doesn't work well for us"
MDD uses Bluetooth, not GPS. GPS doesn't work well indoors where your cars often are (service bays, detail shops, covered lots). Bluetooth provides accurate tracking everywhere on your property.

### "It's too expensive"
Focus on ROI:
- How much time does your team spend searching for keys and cars?
- What's the cost of one lost deal while a customer waits?
- How much do you spend on lost key replacements ($300-$800 per key)?
- The system often pays for itself in the first month.

### "We're too small"
MDD scales from single-point dealerships to the largest publicly traded groups. Same technology Bill Brown Ford uses to track 2,000+ vehicles.
