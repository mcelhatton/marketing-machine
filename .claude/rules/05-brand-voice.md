# Brand Voice — READ THIS FIRST

This is the most important rules file. It governs ALL content tone and style across every channel.

---

## Core Principle

**Dealer language, not vendor language.**

Every sentence should make a dealership operator think "this was written by someone who's been on the lot."

If it sounds like it came from a software vendor's marketing department, rewrite it.

---

## The Saturday Test

Before publishing any content, apply this test:

> Would a GM reading this on their phone between meetings stop and read it?

If not, rewrite.

---

## Forbidden Phrases — NEVER Use These

These are "vendor brochure" phrases that dealership operators ignore:

| Forbidden | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "streamline operations" | Generic vendor speak |
| "enhance efficiency" | Says nothing specific |
| "cutting-edge solution" | Every vendor says this |
| "cutting-edge technology" | Meaningless |
| "innovative technology" | What technology isn't? |
| "leverage our platform" | Corporate jargon |
| "best-in-class" | Unsubstantiated claim |
| "state-of-the-art" | Dated phrase |
| "revolutionize your [anything]" | Hyperbolic |
| "unlock potential" | Vague |
| "unlock new levels" | What levels? |
| "empower your team" | Generic |
| "transform your [anything]" | Overused |
| "seamless integration" | Filler phrase |
| "holistic approach" | Corporate speak |
| "synergy" | Immediate credibility loss |
| "paradigm shift" | Dated jargon |
| "disruptive" | Overused in tech |
| "next-generation" | Says nothing |
| "world-class" | Unsubstantiated |
| "robust solution" | Generic |
| "game-changer" | Hyperbolic |
| "optimize your workflow" | Vendor language |

**The Test:** If the phrase could describe ANY SaaS product without modification, don't use it.

## Required Voice Characteristics

### 1. Scenario-First
Lead with a moment they recognize.

**BAD:** "Our solution improves key management efficiency."

**GOOD:** "It's Saturday at 11:30. A customer is ready to buy. Your salesperson can't find the key."

### 2. Stat-Backed
Every benefit claim must link to a named dealership and a specific number.

**BAD:** "Dealerships see significant improvements."

**GOOD:** "Corwin Toyota cut wait times from 2.5 hours to under 90 minutes."

### 3. Short Sentences
- Social and email: Average 8-12 words per sentence
- Landing pages: Can go longer but keep paragraphs short
- If you can cut a word without losing meaning, cut it

### 4. LocateIQ as the Mechanism
Don't say "our tracking system." Say:

> "LocateIQ — workflow automation powered by real vehicle location."

This positions the unique mechanism that differentiates MDD.

### 5. Demo as CTA
Always drive to demo/consult.

**NEVER use:**
- "Learn more"
- "Download our whitepaper"
- "Contact us"
- "See our pricing page"

**ALWAYS use:**
- "Schedule a 15-Min Demo →"
- "Schedule Your Demo →"
- "Book a Demo"
- "Worth seeing?"

### 6. Conversational Authority
Write like a knowledgeable peer who's spent time on dealership lots, not like a vendor pitching software.

### 7. One Pain Per Piece
Each email, each social post, each ad focuses on ONE specific pain point.

Don't try to cover everything. Go deep on one problem.

### 8. Specific Over General
**BAD:** "Reduce your service wait times significantly."

**GOOD:** "Corwin Toyota cut wait times from 2.5 hours to under 90 minutes."

## Tone by Channel

### Cold Email
- Pattern interrupt subject line
- Under 120 words
- One pain per email
- Soft CTA: "Worth seeing?" or "Curious if something like that would help?" or "Would it be crazy to show you?"
- Never hard-sell

### LinkedIn
- Executive/thought-leadership tone
- Open with an industry insight or provocative stat
- Never sell directly
- Position MDD as the expert, not the vendor
- Use line breaks for readability
- 3-5 hashtags max at bottom

### Facebook
- Visual, conversational, community-oriented
- Dealership culture, team wins, customer stories
- Shorter than LinkedIn
- More casual tone

### Landing Page
- Scannable, stat-heavy
- Structure: problem → solution → proof → CTA
- Dark theme with green accent (per brand)
- Short punchy headlines
- Mobile-first formatting

### Blog
- SEO-friendly, educational
- 800-1200 words
- Positions MDD as thought leaders
- Internal links to product pages
- Answer the searcher's question in the first paragraph

### Press Release
- AP style formatting
- Factual, quote-heavy
- Dateline format
- Boilerplate paragraph at end

### One-Pager / Product Sheet
- Benefit-focused, visual, scannable
- Stats prominent
- Single CTA

## Vocabulary Rules

### Always Use:
| Term | Context |
|------|---------|
| "dealership" | Formal content (not "dealer" alone) |
| "vehicle" | Formal content (not "car") |
| "Locate" (capitalized) | The product name |
| "locate" (lowercase) | The verb |

### Product Names (exact capitalization):
- **LocateIQ** — One word, capital L capital I capital Q. Never "Locate IQ" or "locateiq"
- **VehicleVault** — One word, capital V capital V. Never "Vehicle Vault"
- **Service Workflow** — Two words, both capitalized
- **Recon Workflow** — Two words, both capitalized
- **MDD Locate** — The flagship product

### Dealership Terminology:
| Use | Because |
|-----|---------|
| "bay turns" | Dealer language (not "bay utilization") |
| "RO" | Shorthand for repair order (service managers know this) |
| "CSI" | Customer Satisfaction Index (never spell out) |
| "F&I" | Finance & Insurance (always ampersand, never "and") |
| "DMS" | Dealer Management System (never spell out except first reference) |
| "20 Group" | Peer advisory boards (not tied to a specific number of dealerships) |

### Industry Organizations:
- NADA — National Automobile Dealers Association
- NCM Associates — Popular 20 Group moderator

### Tagline:
> **We Find Keys & Cars™** — Always include the ™

## Anti-Pattern Examples

### Example 1: Key Management
**BAD (vendor language):**
> "Improve operational efficiency with our tracking solution."

**GOOD (dealer language):**
> "It's Saturday at 11:30. A customer is ready to buy. Your salesperson can't find the key."

### Example 2: Service Workflow
**BAD:**
> "Streamline your service operations with our cutting-edge workflow system."

**GOOD:**
> "Corwin Toyota's express service wait times were at 2.5 hours. Now 64% of customers are out within 60 minutes."

### Example 3: Inventory Management
**BAD:**
> "Leverage our innovative technology to enhance inventory management."

**GOOD:**
> "Bill Brown Ford tracks 2,000+ vehicles across multiple lots. Their inventory manager said it eliminated hours of searching every week."

### Example 4: Workflow Accuracy
**BAD:**
> "Our solution provides a holistic approach to dealership efficiency."

**GOOD:**
> "Your workflow dashboard looks organized. But is it accurate? Someone has to remember to log that a car left the bay. They often don't."

### Example 5: F&I Revenue
**BAD:**
> "Unlock new revenue streams with our F&I integration."

**GOOD:**
> "Brandon Honda generates an additional $308K per month selling VehicleVault. Zero chargebacks."

## Writing Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] No forbidden phrases used
- [ ] Scenario-first opening (not feature-first)
- [ ] Specific stats with named dealerships (not vague claims)
- [ ] One pain point per piece (not trying to cover everything)
- [ ] CTA drives to demo (not "learn more")
- [ ] Short sentences and paragraphs
- [ ] Would pass the Saturday Test
- [ ] Dealer language throughout
- [ ] LocateIQ positioned as the mechanism (where relevant)
- [ ] Product names correctly capitalized

## Quick Reference: Good vs. Bad Copy

| Scenario | Bad | Good |
|----------|-----|------|
| Opening hook | "We offer comprehensive tracking..." | "It's Saturday at 11:30..." |
| Feature claim | "Streamlined workflow" | "Automatic updates when vehicles move" |
| Benefit | "Improved efficiency" | "64% of customers out in 60 minutes" |
| CTA | "Learn more about our solution" | "Worth seeing how it works?" |
| Proof | "Many dealerships see results" | "Bill Brown Ford tracks 2,000+ vehicles" |
| Mechanism | "Our advanced technology" | "LocateIQ — automation powered by real vehicle location" |
